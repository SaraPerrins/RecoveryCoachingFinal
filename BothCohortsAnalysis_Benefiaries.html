Drug Use in Past 30 Days

There was a discrepancy between cohorts with item wording where cohort 1 was asked “How often have you used opioids in the last 30 days?” while cohort 2 was asked “How often have you used drugs or alcohol in the last 30 days?” 

There was also a discrepancy with item response choices where cohort 1 had:
1 Once or more times a day
2 A few times per week
3 A few times per month
4 Once a month. 5 I have not used opioids in the last 30 days

Cohort 2 had:
1 Every day
2 At least once a week
3 At least once a month
4 I have not used drugs in the last 30 days

Response 4 in C1 is unique to C1. Since Cohort 1 responses only had 1 or 5 responses, it is not a problem to remove option 4 from C1. The reconciled response scale will have 4 choices: Every day, At least once/week, At least once/month, and I have not used drugs in the last 30 days. Limitation in interpretation is that C1 asked only about opioids, while C2 asked about drugs.

Physical/Behavioral Services

Cohort 1 and Cohort 2 had differing response scales:
C1: “Since entering recovery, how often have you utilized physical and/or behavioral health services on average? - Selected Choice” 1 Daily 2 Once per week or more 3 One or two times a month 4 A couple times a year 5 Less than two times a year 6 I do not attend health services 7 Other (fill-in)

C2: Since entering recovery, how often have you used physical and/or behavioral health services on average? 1 Every day 2 At least once a week 3 At least once a month 4 At least once a year 5 I do not attend health services

Response options 5 and 7 are unique to C1. However, no C1 respondent said “5”, so that can be omitted as a response choice (i.e., We can recode options 5 and 6 to become “I do not attend health services”.) N = 4 responses had “7 Other”. We will can now combine C1 and C2 to have options 1-7 (where 5 and 6 are “I do not attend services”, and 7 remains as Other. The limitation is that we didn’t give C2 respondents a chance to report “Other”).
